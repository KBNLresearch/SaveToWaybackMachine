Littera cursiva

A new script that developed at the end of the 12th century, when book-hands were reduced to simpler forms, is called cursive. Cursive letters are interconnected, written without lifting the pen. As a consequence cursive script has loops.

Cursive scripts in the Low Countries appear in different forms: littera cursiva (A), with a 'double' a; littera cursiva (C), with a 'simple' a; the transitional littera cursiva (A/C), with both 'simple' and 'double' a; and littera cursiva (C/H), a cursive with hybrid characteristics (or a hybrid with cursive characteristics). There is also regional variation: English cursive script is known as Anglicana, and a specific cursive written in Franceand the Low Countriesis referred to as Bourguignonne.
